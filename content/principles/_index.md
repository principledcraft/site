Software exists to help people. That sounds simple, but keeping it true over time takes discipline. Code rots, complexity creeps in, and teams slow down without always noticing why. 
These pages focus on principles over recipes. The goal is to understand *why* something matters so you can apply it to your own context, not to follow rules blindly.

As I learn, read or discover more, these pages will no doubt evolve and update over time, so stay tuned!

**[Quality](/principles/quality/)** covers the big picture: why software should be simple, what poor quality actually costs, and how to protect the developer experience that keeps teams productive.

**[Testing](/principles/testing/)** covers how to test well, from what makes a good test through to structuring code so it is easy to test in the first place.

### On the way...

*Writing better code* will focus on writing code that is simpler, more readable, and easier to maintain over time.

*Beyond the code* will cover the practices around the code itself: things that shape how well you and your team actually deliver.
