<p class="book-meta">Max Kanat-Alexander &nbsp;·&nbsp; Packt &nbsp;·&nbsp; September 2017 &nbsp;·&nbsp; <a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/1788628810">Available here</a></p>

Forty-three essays on simplicity, coding, and how to improve as a developer. Drawing on experience with Bugzilla and open source, it covers debugging, code complexity, team dynamics, and career growth. The essays are short and self-contained, so you can read them in any order. It's a practical companion to the author's earlier book, [Code Simplicity](/books/code-simplicity), expanding those ideas into everyday development situations.
